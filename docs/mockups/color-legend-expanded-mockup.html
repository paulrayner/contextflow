Our Team
Our Team
Your team builds and owns this bounded context.
  • • You control the roadmap and priorities
  • • Your team maintains the codebase
  • • Changes without external coordination
Internal (Other Team)
Internal (Other Team)
Another team within your organization owns this.
  • • Requires coordination for changes
  • • You may be a consumer/downstream
  • • Internal SLAs and contracts apply
External (Third Party)
External (Third Party)
A third-party or external organization owns this.
  • • Limited ability to request changes
  • • Typically requires anti-corruption layer
  • • Subject to external contracts/SLAs
Core Domain
Core Domain
What makes your business unique. Invest heavily here.
  • • Source of competitive advantage
  • • Build and maintain in-house
  • • Attract your best talent here
Supporting Subdomain
Supporting Subdomain
Necessary but not a differentiator.
  • • Supports core domain functionality
  • • Consider outsourcing if complex
  • • Keep simple where possible
Generic Subdomain
Generic Subdomain
Common capabilities many businesses need.
  • • Not unique to your business
  • • Buy, rent, or use open source
  • • Minimize custom development
Border Style
Solid — Internal system
Solid Border
Systems your organization owns and controls.
Dashed — External system
Dashed Border

Border Thickness
Thick — Strong boundary
Strong Boundary
Well-defined interface with strict contracts.
  • • Clear API contracts
  • • Versioned interfaces
  • • Independent deployability
Medium — Moderate boundary
Moderate Boundary
Defined interface but some coupling exists.
  • • Some shared dependencies
  • • Coordination required for changes
  • • Working toward stronger boundary
Thin dotted — Weak boundary
Weak Boundary
Significant coupling with other contexts.
  • • Shared database or models
  • • Changes ripple across contexts
  • • Consider strengthening over time
